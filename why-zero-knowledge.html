Security & privacy

Private by math, not by promise.

Aster is built so you don't have to trust us. Capture stays on your Mac, and what you share is encrypted so that no one, not even us, can read it. Not a privacy policy you have to believe, a guarantee the cryptography enforces.

The problem

Ambient AI created a trust problem

The last wave of meeting and memory tools made a quiet trade: to be useful, they put a continuous record of your work, your calls, and your screen on someone else's servers, and often used it to train their models. Bots joined calls as silent participants. It worked, until everyone realized how much was leaving their control.

The rules are catching up. More than a dozen US states require all parties to consent before a conversation is recorded, and no court has accepted a bot's presence in a participant list as valid consent. Regulators are scrutinizing these tools under GDPR, HIPAA, and CCPA, and professional-conduct bodies have warned that recording without strict consent and data controls can breach duties of confidentiality. Compliance and legal teams responded the obvious way: they started blocking them.

How Aster is different

Built so trust isn't required

Bot-free by design

No bot joins your meetings. Aster captures your screen and the computer's own audio locally on your Mac. There's no uninvited participant in the call, and no recording sitting on a vendor's server by default.

Zero-knowledge sharing

When you share, the content is encrypted on your Mac before it leaves. The key travels in the link itself and is never sent to our servers; we store only ciphertext. We cannot read your shares, and a breach of us reveals nothing.

Your keys, your models

Summarize on-device with Apple Intelligence, Qwen, or Gemma 4 E4B, or bring your own Anthropic or OpenAI key and call frontier models directly, no proxy in between. Nothing you record is used to train anyone's model.

For teams

Built to pass a security review

Most privacy-first tools force a choice: trust a vendor's cloud, or run everything yourself. Aster's team layer is designed to be both managed and zero-knowledge, so individuals get a real product and security teams get an answer they can sign off on.

Zero-knowledge workspaceSoon

Shared team spaces where content is encrypted to each member's key. The server stores ciphertext and brokers keys; by default it can't read a thing.

Opt-in Cloud AISoon

Cross-meeting search and summaries are off by default. A workspace turns them on deliberately, with a zero-day retention option that keeps only the summary and discards the source.

Two kinds of trust

Transparency is good. Math is better.

Cloud tools

Ask you to trust their servers, their staff, and their training policy. The data is readable; you're trusting a promise not to look.

Self-hosted & open source

Ask you to audit the code and run your own server. Strong, if you're an engineer with the time. Most people, and most teams, aren't.

Aster

Asks neither. What you share is encrypted so it can't be read, and capture stays on your Mac. You don't have to read our code or run a server, the cryptography guarantees it.

The honest version: zero-knowledge isn't a compliance certificate. For regulated work, teams still need signed agreements and audited controls, which is why a BAA and SOC 2 are on our roadmap. But it does remove the thing every other tool asks you to do: take their word for it.
